The Generation Gap

A mixed academic + creative exploration of causes, effects and practical ways to bridge generational differences.

What is the Generation Gap?

The generation gap refers to differences in values, communication styles, technology use and expectations between age groups. These differences emerge from historical events, economic conditions, and cultural shifts experienced during formative years.

Psychological perspective

Psychologists describe how "impressionable years" shape long-term beliefs. Events and media consumed during adolescence often anchor attitudes that persist into adulthood.

Sociological perspective

Sociology highlights structural changes (education, labor markets, migration) that influence generational identities and intergenerational power dynamics.

Workplace & values

Expectations about work-life balance, career paths, and leadership often differ sharply between older and younger workers, producing both tension and innovation.

  • Work: Remote-first roles and freelance work versus stable long-term employment.

Bridging tips

Open dialogue, mentorship programs, and shared projects are practical ways to turn gaps into strengths.

  1. Create mentorship pairings across age groups.
  2. Prioritize listening over fixing.
  3. Encourage shared learning sessions (technology clinics, storytelling hours).
